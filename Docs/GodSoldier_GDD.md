# God Soldier Game Design

## Pillars
- Asymmetric cooperation: the God and Soldier must solve different halves of the same problem.
- Spiritual vs physical play: the God bends meaning, timing, and support space; the Soldier resolves material obstacles and combat.
- Moral tension: the story argues `Order vs Agency`, not simple good vs evil.
- Premium framing: menu, mission selection, and pre-match flow should feel deliberate and cinematic.

## Early Campaign Structure
- Mission 1 `Descent`
- Mission 2 `War Trial`
- Mission 3 `Judgment`

## Mission 1: Descent
- Opening cutscene: the God descends through war and revives the fallen Soldier.
- Revival minigame: both players must perform synchronized input to restore motion.
- Chamber investigation: the God reveals hidden clues while the Soldier searches the room physically.
- Obstacle beat: both players push through a heavy obstruction together.
- Dream traversal: the God stabilizes temporary platforms while the Soldier parkours forward.
- Physical-realm combat: the Soldier reaches the first firearm encounter.
- Resource phase: the Soldier gathers explosive materials under the God’s instruction.
- Finale: the God crafts the explosive and the Soldier uses it to break the final breach.

## Mission 2: War Trial
- Co-op scripted boss mission, not PvP.
- Enemy duo:
- false God
- corrupted Soldier
- Encounter rhythm:
- major boss telegraph phase
- cooperative counter window
- exposed boss damage phase
- filler enemy wave or regroup beat
- repeat with the second boss
- Desired feel: a raid-like duel where cooperation is mandatory for counterplay.

## Mission 3: Judgment
- Slower, story-led mission.
- Chamber-based decisions escalate the `Order vs Agency` score.
- The assassination concept is folded into the final choice instead of becoming a separate mission.
- Endings:
- imposed peace
- earned freedom
- uneasy verdict

## Role Fantasy
- God:
- reveal clues
- stabilize traversal
- support, stun, mark, and craft
- interpret war from above
- Soldier:
- move, jump, parkour, push, carry
- shoot and survive
- execute the physical half of every breakthrough

## Menu And Structure
- Main menu:
- `Play`
- `Settings`
- `Exit`
- After `Play`, the player sees the mission timeline.
- All missions are visible from first boot.
- Out-of-order play is allowed, but the game warns politely and recommends the earlier mission.

## Scope Guardrails
- Current launch-plan structure is `2-player co-op only`.
- No PvP in the active early-game plan.
- No open-world hub yet.
- No extra playable roles.
- Final graphics, cinematics, and art polish remain a later pass after systems stability.
